# Sprint 096 — BW Threshold Mechanism: Entanglement Spectrum at the Breakdown

## Hypothesis
BW breaks down at nA=6 for q=2 (Sprint 095). Why? The entanglement spectrum tail weight grows with nA, and at some critical tail fraction, the BW approximation (which assumes a local Hamiltonian) can no longer capture the non-local correlations encoded in the tail. This sprint connects the two major threads: entanglement spectrum (Sprints 087-090) and BW fidelity (Sprints 091-095).

## Experiments

### 096a — Entanglement spectrum vs nA at fixed n (q=2)
q=2 periodic, n=14. Compute entanglement spectrum (Schmidt spectrum of ground state) for nA=3,4,5,6,7. Track: tail weight, entanglement gap, number of significant eigenvalues, BW R². Correlate.

### 096b — Same for q=5 (lower threshold)
q=5 periodic, n=8, nA=3,4. BW threshold is at nA=4 for q=5. Does the same spectrum property predict breakdown?

### 096c — Compilation
Identify which entanglement spectrum property universally predicts BW breakdown across q.

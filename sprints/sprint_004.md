# Sprint 004 — Quantum Mutual Information & Integrated Information

**Date:** 2026-03-31
**Status:** In progress

## Idea

Entanglement entropy is a single number per bipartition — too coarse (Sprint 002c proved this). More nuanced measures:

1. **Quantum Mutual Information** I(A:B) = S(A) + S(B) - S(AB) captures total correlations (classical + quantum)
2. **Tripartite mutual information** I3 = I(A:B) + I(A:C) - I(A:BC) — negative values indicate genuinely multipartite entanglement
3. **Integrated information (Phi)** from IIT — measures how much a system is "more than the sum of its parts"

We'll compute these for our state zoo (GHZ, W, Cluster) at small scales and see if they distinguish the states better than entropy alone.

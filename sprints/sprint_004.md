# Sprint 004 — Quantum Mutual Information & Integrated Information

**Date:** 2026-03-31
**Status:** Complete

## Idea

Entanglement entropy is a single number per bipartition — too coarse (Sprint 002c proved this). More nuanced measures:

1. **Quantum Mutual Information** I(A:B) = S(A) + S(B) - S(AB) captures total correlations (classical + quantum)
2. **Tripartite mutual information** I3 = I(A:B) + I(A:C) - I(A:BC) — negative values indicate genuinely multipartite entanglement
3. **Integrated information (Phi)** from IIT — measures how much a system is "more than the sum of its parts"

We'll compute these for our state zoo (GHZ, W, Cluster) at small scales and see if they distinguish the states better than entropy alone.

## Experiments

### 4a: Quantum Mutual Information Matrix (6 qubits)

Computed pairwise MI I(i:j) = S(i) + S(j) - S(ij) for all pairs in each state.

**GHZ MI matrix:** Uniform 1.0 everywhere. Every qubit pair is maximally correlated.
**W MI matrix:** Uniform 0.38 everywhere. Democratic but weak correlations.
**Cluster MI matrix:** Sparse — only nearest neighbors have MI=1.0, all others exactly 0.0.

| Metric | GHZ | W | Cluster |
|--------|-----|-------|---------|
| Total pairwise MI | 15.0 | 5.73 | 2.0 |
| Mean pair MI | 1.000 | 0.382 | 0.133 |
| Std pair MI | 0.000 | 0.000 | 0.340 |

**Key insight:** MI finally distinguishes the three state families. Cluster states have *local* correlations (CZ structure visible), GHZ is *global* (all-to-all), W is *uniform but weak*.

### 4b: Tripartite Mutual Information (6 qubits, all 20 triples)

I3(A:B:C) = S(A)+S(B)+S(C) - S(AB)-S(AC)-S(BC) + S(ABC)

| State | I3 range | Mean I3 | Negative count | Interpretation |
|-------|----------|---------|----------------|---------------|
| GHZ | [+1.0, +1.0] | +1.0 | 0/20 | Purely redundant (classical-like) |
| W | [+0.195, +0.195] | +0.195 | 0/20 | Weakly redundant |
| Cluster | [**-1.0**, 0.0] | -0.3 | **6/20** | Genuinely tripartite quantum |

**This is the most informative result yet.**

- **Positive I3 (GHZ, W):** Information is redundant across triples. Knowing two qubits constrains the third. For GHZ, this is maximal — it's basically a classical broadcast channel in the Z basis.
- **Negative I3 (Cluster):** Irreducible three-body correlations that cannot be decomposed into pairwise ones. This is a signature of genuine multipartite quantum entanglement.
- The 6 negative-I3 triples in the cluster state are all **consecutive triples** on the chain: (0,1,2), (1,2,3), (2,3,4), (3,4,5), and their variants. These are exactly the triples where the CZ-gate connectivity creates non-local 3-body quantum structure.

## Analysis

We now have a hierarchy of measures, each revealing more structure:

1. **Half-cut entropy:** Same (1.0) for GHZ, W, Cluster — useless for distinguishing them
2. **Single-qubit entropy:** Distinguishes GHZ/Cluster (1.0) from W (decreasing with n)
3. **Pairwise mutual information:** Distinguishes all three — GHZ (uniform high), W (uniform low), Cluster (sparse local)
4. **Tripartite information:** The deepest distinction — GHZ is redundant, Cluster has irreducible 3-body quantum correlations

The tripartite information connects directly to quantum error correction and holography: states with negative I3 have information that is "spread" across subsystems in a way that no pair of subsystems can recover. This is the operational meaning of genuine multipartite entanglement.

## What Surprised Me

1. GHZ has POSITIVE I3 despite being a paradigmatic entangled state. Its correlations are structured like a classical broadcast — they're just in a superposition basis.
2. Cluster state negative I3 occurs only for consecutive triples — the geometry of the state is directly visible in the information-theoretic measure.
3. W state is surprisingly boring from an information-theory perspective — uniform everything, no structure.
